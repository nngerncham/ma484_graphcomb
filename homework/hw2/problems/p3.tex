\question

  Let $T$ be a tree of order $n$ and size $m$ having $n_i$ vertices of degree 
  $i$ for $i \geq 1$. Then $n=\sum_i n_i$ and $2(n-1)=2 m=\sum_i i \cdot n_i$.

  \begin{parts}
    \part Prove that $n_1=2+n_3+2 n_4+3 n_5+4 n_6+\ldots$.

      \begin{solution}
        Abusing notation, let \(\Delta\) denote the maximum degree of the graph.
        Consider
        \[
          \begin{aligned}
            2(n - 1) &= n_1 + 2n_2 + 3n_3 + \cdots + \Delta n_\Delta \\
            2(n_1 + n_2 + n_3 + \cdots + n_\Delta - 1)
                      &= n_1 + 2n_2 + 3n_3 + \cdots + \Delta n_\Delta \\
            2n_1 + 2n_2 + n_3 + \cdots + 2n_\Delta - 2
                      &= n_1 + 2n_2 + 3n_3 + \cdots + \Delta n_\Delta \\
            \cancelto{1}{2}n_1 + 2n_2 + n_3 + \cdots + 2n_\Delta - 2 
                      &= \cancel{n_1} + 2n_2 + 3n_3 + \cdots + \Delta n_\Delta
                      \\ 
            n_1 + \cancel{2n_2} + \cancel{2n_3} + \cdots + \cancel{2n_\Delta}
            \cancel{-2}
                      &= \cancel{2n_2} + \cancelto{1}{3}n_3 + \cdots +
                      (\Delta - 2)n_\Delta + 2 \\ 
            n_1       &= 2 + n_3 + 2n_4 + 3n_5 + \cdots + (\Delta-2)n_\Delta
          \end{aligned}
        \]
        Thus, we have that \(n_1 = 2 + n_3 + 2n_4 + 3n_5 + \cdots + (\Delta -
        2)n_\Delta\).
      \end{solution}

    \part A tree $T$ has three vertices of degree 2, five vertices of degree 3,
      two vertices of degree 4 and no vertices of degree 5 or more. According to
      the formula in (a), how many end vertices does $T$ have?

      \begin{solution}
        From the problem, we can identify the following. 
        \[
          \begin{aligned}
            n_2 &= 3 \\ 
            n_3 &= 5 \\ 
            n_4 &= 2
          \end{aligned}
        \]
        Then, we have that 
        \[
          \begin{aligned}
            n_1 &= 2 + n_3 + 2n_4 \\ 
                &= 2 + 5 + 2(2) \\ 
                &= 11
          \end{aligned}
        \]
        We also have that
        \[
          \begin{aligned}
            n &= n_1 + n_2 + n_3 + n_4 \\ 
              &= 11 + 3 + 5 + 2 \\ 
              &= 21
          \end{aligned}
        \]
        Thus, there are 11 end vertices and 21 vertices in total. 
      \end{solution}
  \end{parts}
